Vote for Natural Language Processing Talks at SXSW!

Ok, I suppose what I mean is, vote for my natural language processing talks at South by Southwest (SXSW), a set of film, music, and geek-a-rrific "interactive" festivals and conferences held annually in Austin, Texas.

South by Southwest has a great mix of different attendees and it's a wonderful opportunity to get the word out about language technology, so this year I've pulled out all the stops and I'm involved in several proposals, each of which has its own distinct message. Popular voting counts for about 30% when proposals are selected, so your vote matters! (And yes, the organizers permit and even encourage get-out-the-vote-for-yourself campaigns.)

The way this works:

  • You get an account at the SXSW PanelPicker 2012 site by going to the Create an Account page, which takes all of about 30 seconds.
  • Then, go to the link below that interests you, read through the full description, and (I hope) give it a thumbs up where it says "Your Vote" (just under the title).
Better yet, go give a thumbs up for all of these talks! At 30 seconds apiece, that's a total of two minutes, devoted to an excellent cause.

Voting ends Friday, September 2, 2011 -- vote now!

  • Human Language Technology and Where it's Headed
    Jason Baldridge and I are aiming to give a fun, informative presentation on the state of the art in language technology, why these are such exciting times, and what's coming. It's time the broader techie audience got an earful about why our field is so cool!

  • EHRs, NLP, and the Future of Clinical Narrative
    After decades of failed efforts to make medical records electronic, it now looks like electronic health records (EHRs)are finally on the verge of catching on in this country for real, thanks to government incentives for doctors who use them and, in a couple of years, penalties levied on the doctors who don't. The problem in a nutshell: in a well intentioned drive toward standardization and normalization, the people creating EHRs are driving natural language out of the clinical record. My goal in this talk is to raise awareness of the problem, and to show how natural language processing technology provides a solution.

  • Artificial Intelligence in Film and the Real World
    And now for something completely different: computer scientists proposing a presentation at a film festival?! Lillian Lee and I thought it would be great fun to use portrayals of artificial intelligence in the movies as a springboard for helping non-techies understand what AI really is, and also to tell the artsy and business types about AI research involving films, e.g. predicting box office revenues using early reviews and computational linguistics analysis of film scripts.

Finally, let me suggest you also vote for this really interesting panel discussion including Mark Dredze, focused on the use of social media to improve healthcare. Perhaps I risk shooting myself in the foot by pointing folks to a proposal competing in one of the same categories, but we NLP folks should be supporting each other!
