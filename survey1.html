Instructions

This study is looking at the quality of translations between English and French. The list below includes examples of translation from a variety of sources, including translations done by professional translators, by people who aren't professionals, and by machines. There is no way of knowing for sure whether the original text was in English or in French, so the English is always listed first.

For each item on the list, you are asked to provide three ratings.

  1. Quality of the English.
    • 3 -- Very fluent: all of the English is comprehensible.
    • 2 -- Fairly fluent: the major part of the English passes but there are noticable errors.
    • 1 -- Barely fluent: only part of the English meaning is understandable.
    • 0 -- Unintelligible: nothing or almost nothing of the English is comprehensible.

  2. Quality of the French.
    • 3 -- Very fluent: all of the French is comprehensible.
    • 2 -- Fairly fluent: the major part of the French passes but there are noticable errors.
    • 1 -- Barely fluent: only part of the French meaning is understandable.
    • 0 -- Unintelligible: nothing or almost nothing of the French is comprehensible.

  3. Adequacy of translation.
    • 4 -- The English and French contain entirely the same meaning.
    • 3 -- The English and French contain mostly the same meaning.
    • 2 -- The English and French contain much of the same meaning.
    • 1 -- The English and French contain little of the same meaning.
    • 0 -- The English and French contain none of the same meaning.
    Notice that for this one, the top score is 4, not 3!

There are three ways to return your answers.

  1. Use the spreadsheet I've provided for you. Fill in the three columns for each item, save the spreadsheet, and mail it to: resnik@umd.edu

  2. Create a file with a line for each item, showing 
           Item_number  English_fluency  French_fluency  Translation_Adequacy
    Plain text or Microsoft Word files are both fine. Mail to: resnik@umd.edu

  3. Write your answers down on paper and slip it under my office door, AV Williams 3142.
Thank you very much for your help!
